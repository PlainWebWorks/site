Greenwood Baptist Church — Digital Presence Proposal

Your sermons deserve more than a Facebook page.

Right now, someone in Lincoln County searching for a church finds nothing for Greenwood Baptist. No website. No Google listing. No phone number online. This proposal outlines how to fix all of that — at no cost to the church — and what that unlocks long term.

The current situation

Greenwood Baptist Church exists online only as a directory listing on the Georgia Baptist Association website. That listing has an address and service times — but no phone number, no email, no way for anyone to contact the church directly.

Sermons are posted to Facebook, which means they are only visible to people already on Facebook, and they disappear into a feed. They are not searchable. They are not permanent. They do not reach anyone outside the congregation.

What gets set up

  • 1
    greenwood-baptist.com A clean, simple website with service times, location, contact information, and a brief description of the church. Mobile-friendly and fast.
  • 2
    Church email address A professional address like pastor@greenwood-baptist.com that belongs to the church — not to any individual.
  • 3
    Google Business Profile Greenwood Baptist appears when someone searches for churches in the area. Shows hours, address, phone, and links to the website.
  • 4
    YouTube channel for sermons Sermons posted to YouTube are searchable, permanent, and reachable by anyone — including members who miss a Sunday and family outside the area.

Cost to the church

Domain (greenwood-baptist.com) $0
Google Workspace (email, Drive, Meet) $0
Website setup and design $0
Google Business Profile setup $0
YouTube nonprofit channel setup $0

Google provides free Workspace accounts to verified 501(c)(3) organizations through the Google for Nonprofits program. The domain is being donated as a gift to the church. Ongoing domain renewal is approximately $10 per year.

The church owns everything

The domain, the email accounts, the website, and every Google account are registered to Greenwood Baptist Church — not to any individual. If leadership changes, if a new pastor comes, if the church ever wants to manage this independently, everything transfers cleanly. No mystery logins. No dependency on one person.

In return, permission is requested to reference the church's website as an example of work done by Plain Web Works — a local small business web service.

A note on sermons and YouTube

Facebook keeps content inside Facebook. A sermon posted there is invisible to anyone not already in the congregation. YouTube is indexed by Google — a sermon posted there can be found by anyone searching, watched by family members across the country, and shared outside the church community. The YouTube Nonprofit Program also allows the church to add a donation button directly to videos.
