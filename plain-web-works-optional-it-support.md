# Plain Web Works — Optional IT Support

This is a draft idea for offering general IT support when a website or online-presence client starts needing broader help.

The goal is simple: Plain Web Works can help with IT problems, but website work and general IT support are separate services.

## Basic Positioning

Plain Web Works is primarily a small-business web presence service: websites, domains, DNS, business email setup, Google Business Profiles, contact forms, reviews, and basic security.

Some clients may also need help with general technology issues. That can be offered separately as hourly IT support.

The important boundary:

> Website packages do not include general computer, printer, Wi-Fi, phone, Microsoft/Google Workspace, malware cleanup, or employee device support. Those are billed separately as IT support.

## When IT Support Applies

IT support applies when the client asks for help with things outside the website package, such as:

- Computers, laptops, or workstations
- Printers and scanners
- Wi-Fi or router issues
- Basic network troubleshooting
- Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace account issues
- Email client setup
- Password manager setup
- MFA setup
- Phone or tablet configuration
- Basic backup guidance
- Malware popups or suspicious device behavior
- Employee login or access problems
- Software installation or configuration
- General “my computer is acting weird” problems

## Starting Rates

These are working numbers and can be adjusted later.

| Service Type | Rate | Minimum |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Remote IT support | $125/hr | 1 hour |
| Onsite IT support | $150/hr | 2 hours |
| After-hours / urgent support | $225/hr | 2 hours |
| Security incident / suspected compromise | Starting at $200/hr | Quoted separately |

## Remote Support

Remote support is best for:

- Account setup
- Email client setup
- Password resets
- MFA setup
- DNS/email record checks
- Software configuration
- Basic troubleshooting
- Guided cleanup
- Google Workspace / Microsoft 365 support
- Website-adjacent issues

Remote support minimum: **1 hour**.

## Onsite Support

Onsite support is best for:

- Printer issues
- Wi-Fi/router issues
- Physical device setup
- Cabling checks
- Workstation setup
- Small-office troubleshooting
- Problems that cannot be handled remotely

Onsite support minimum: **2 hours**.

This minimum matters because travel, setup time, client conversation, and follow-up can easily eat half a day.

## After-Hours / Urgent Support

After-hours or urgent support should cost more because it interrupts other work and creates higher expectations.

Use this category for:

- Same-day emergency requests
- Evening/weekend work
- “Everything is down” situations
- Time-sensitive account access issues
- Urgent email/domain problems
- Last-minute business-critical troubleshooting

Suggested rate: **$225/hr, 2-hour minimum**.

## Security Incidents

Do not casually absorb security incidents into normal support.

A suspected compromise can become messy fast. It may involve business email compromise, malware, stolen passwords, fraudulent invoices, unauthorized account access, customer data, legal concerns, or insurance reporting.

Use separate pricing and scope for:

- Suspected malware
- Business email compromise
- Unauthorized account access
- Phishing response
- Credential cleanup
- Suspicious financial activity
- Compromised website/domain/email accounts
- Employee account takeover
- Device compromise

Suggested language:

> Security incidents are handled separately from normal IT support. The starting rate is $200/hr, and the work is scoped based on what happened, what systems are involved, and how urgent the response is.

## No Unlimited Support by Default

Do not offer unlimited “call me whenever” support casually.

That creates weird expectations and turns into unpaid helpdesk work.

Better default language:

> I offer hourly IT support as needed. If you end up needing regular help every month, we can talk about a support agreement later.

## Possible Future Retainer

A retainer may make sense later, but it should be tested carefully.

Possible starter version:

| Plan | Monthly Price | Included Time | Extra Time |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Basic IT Support | $500/mo | Up to 3 remote hours | $125/hr |
| Small Office Support | $900/mo | Up to 6 remote hours | $125/hr |

Potential rules:

- Unused hours do not roll over.
- Onsite work is billed separately or consumes time at the onsite rate.
- Emergency response is not guaranteed unless explicitly included.
- Support covers agreed systems only.
- Hardware, software licenses, subscriptions, and third-party fees are not included.
- Security incidents are not included unless explicitly scoped.

For now, retainers should be treated as experimental, not the default offer.

## Client-Facing Explanation

Use this when a website client starts asking for broader IT help:

> I can help with that, but it is outside the website package. Website work covers the site, domain, DNS, basic email records, and related online-presence setup. General computer, printer, Wi-Fi, account, device, or employee support is billed separately as IT support.
>
> My IT support rate is $125/hr remote with a 1-hour minimum, or $150/hr onsite with a 2-hour minimum. After-hours or urgent support is $225/hr with a 2-hour minimum.

## Short Version

> Plain Web Works can provide general IT support when needed, but it is separate from website work. Remote support is $125/hr with a 1-hour minimum. Onsite support is $150/hr with a 2-hour minimum. After-hours or urgent support is $225/hr with a 2-hour minimum. Security incidents are scoped separately.

## Internal Rule

Do not let website clients accidentally turn into unpaid IT clients.

If the work involves their website, domain, DNS, business email records, Google listing, or contact form, it may fit the web package.

If the work involves computers, printers, Wi-Fi, employee devices, Microsoft/Google accounts, malware, backups, or general troubleshooting, it is IT support.

Bill accordingly.
